Frequently Asked Questions

Can I watch every college football game for free?
Yes — free streaming sites like SportsSurge carry every game from every conference. For a legal option, games on ABC are free over-the-air with an antenna. ESPN+ at $10.99/month covers most games not on major networks.
What's the cheapest way to watch all college football?
NorthStream IPTV at $12.99/month covers every game across all conferences plus bowl games. For official services, YouTube TV at $72.99/month is the only single service that carries all major conference networks.
How do I watch the College Football Playoff?
The 12-team College Football Playoff (starting 2026) will be broadcast across ESPN, ABC, and TNT. First-round games are on ESPN/ABC, quarterfinals on ESPN, and the semifinals and championship on ESPN. Free sites and IPTV carry all games.
Can I watch college football on my phone?
Yes — ESPN app, YouTube TV app, and Peacock all work on iOS/Android. Free sites work in mobile browsers. IPTV services offer mobile apps. YouTube TV's mobile app is especially good with unlimited cloud DVR.
What about bowl games and rivalry week?
Bowl games are spread across ESPN, ABC, and CBS. Rivalry Week (last week of November) features the biggest matchups on major networks. IPTV and free sites carry all bowl games. ESPN+ does NOT include major bowl games — you need a service with ESPN proper.
